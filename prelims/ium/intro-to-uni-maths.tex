\begin{columns}
  \column{0.5}
  \block{Induction}{
    Induction is a very general principle within proof that uses characteristics of a problem whereby it is easier to reason about how the former case varies from the next than each individual case. One often first sees induction as defined in terms of the natural numbers, although this isn't the general case.\\

    \innerblock{Natural Induction}{
    Given the family of propositions $P_{n}$ for some $n \in \mathbb{N}$, if $P_{0}$ is true and for any $k \in \mathbb{N}$ we have $P_{k}$ implies $P_{k+1}$, then for all $n \in \mathbb{N}$ we have $P_{n}$ true.
    }
